Question: What happened to the economy the first time the government raised the minimum wage ? Did the economy perform poorly , better or no change ? Did employment fall or rise ? Did the number of people in poverty increase or decrease ? Please explain like I'm five.

Answer: It's difficult to say exactly what happened to the economy the first time the government raised the minimum wage, because there have been many instances of the minimum wage being increased over the years, and the effects of these increases can vary depending on a variety of factors. In general, raising the minimum wage can have both positive and negative effects on the economy and on employment. On the positive side, raising the minimum wage can increase the incomes of low-wage workers, which can lead to increased spending and a boost to economic activity. This can also lead to a reduction in poverty, as low-wage workers are able to afford a higher standard of living. On the negative side, raising the minimum wage can also lead to an increase in the cost of doing business for employers, which can lead to reduced profits and potentially even cause some businesses to close. This can lead to a decrease in employment, as businesses may be unable to afford to hire as many workers. Overall, the impact of raising the minimum wage on the economy and on employment can be complex and depends on a variety of factors. It's important to consider all of the potential consequences before making a decision about whether to raise the minimum wage or not.
